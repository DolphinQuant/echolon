## Acceptable use (no permission needed)

You may use the Echolon name in these ways without asking:

- **Mentioning** Echolon in articles, blog posts, talks, comparison content, academic papers, social media, or any factual reference.
- **"Built with Echolon"** or "Powered by Echolon" badges on tools, projects, or services that use Echolon as a dependency.
- **`echolon-<thing>` PyPI package names** for community extensions, integrations, and tooling that work with Echolon (e.g. `echolon-cme-adapter`, `echolon-vectorbt-bridge`).
- **`<thing>-for-echolon`** naming for dedicated integrations.
- **Forking** the repository under a clearly different name. The fork's name must not include "Echolon".
- **Linking** to <https://github.com/dolphinquant/echolon> from documentation, tutorials, or marketing.

## Not acceptable without written permission

The following uses require permission from DolphinQuant:

- Naming a fork or derivative product **"Echolon"**, **"Echolon Pro"**, **"Echolon Enterprise"**, **"Echolon Cloud"**, or any variant that could be confused with the official project.
- Using the **Echolon logo** for a commercial product, service, or marketing material.
- Implying **official endorsement** by DolphinQuant or the Echolon project (e.g. "Echolon-certified", "official Echolon partner").
- Domain names of the form **`echolon.<tld>`**, **`echolon-<anything>.<tld>`** (e.g. `echolon-pro.com`, `echolonai.io`), or domains that could plausibly be mistaken for the official project's site.
- **Commercial training, certification, or consulting** services using the Echolon name without an authorization agreement.

## Why this exists

Apache 2.0 is the right license for the *code*. It explicitly does not give you the right to use the project's *name* in ways that could confuse users about authorship or quality. This separation is standard practice for open-source projects with active brands — Linux, Python, Kubernetes, Mozilla, and many others have similar policies.

Practically: anyone can read the code, fork it, modify it, ship a competing product on top of it, and sell that product commercially. What they can't do is call their commercial product "Echolon" or imply it's the same thing.
